Baseball Camera Angle Annotation Guidelines

A position-and-purpose based taxonomy for classifying camera angles in baseball broadcast footage.

The 8 Categories

Category 1

Center Field Cam (Main Pitch Cam)

Position
Center field, telephoto lens, shooting straight down the pitcher–batter line.
Purpose
Default broadcast angle. Shows pitch type, trajectory, and strike zone calls. Occupies the majority of live broadcast time.
Tag when
Pitcher and batter appear aligned/overlapping in frame, catcher visible behind batter.
Example

Full battery view — pitcher, batter, catcher, and umpire all visible in one frame.

Category 2

High Home Wide (Press Box Wide)

Position
Press box level, elevated above home plate.
Purpose
Shows full infield formation, defensive shifts, baserunner leads, overall field state.
Tag when
All infielders visible in a single wide frame; ball/play position secondary to formation.
Example

Full infield with runner leading off first base, pitcher and umpires visible.

Category 3

Low Home (Backstop / Reaction Tight)

Position
Assumed: behind backstop netting, low height. Possibly the same camera as Category 1 (Center Field Cam), zoomed in — not yet confirmed.
Purpose
Batter/pitcher facial reaction, close-up on mitt position, between-pitch moments.
Tag when
Single person fills most of frame (face/shoulders), background heavily blurred/compressed, no play in progress.
Examples

Batter reaction, between pitches. Note the compressed/blurred crowd background.

Pitcher close-up on the mound. Same compressed background as the batter shot.

Category 4

Baseline Cam (Infield Tag/Base Plays)

Position
Along any baseline or at any infield base (1st, 2nd, or 3rd), low angle, close to the play.
Purpose
Tag plays, stolen bases, bunts, pickoff attempts, runner slides — at any base, not just the corners.
Tag when
Base and runner shown low-angle, from the side or front, close proximity.
Examples

Low angle along the third base line, ball on the ground near the foul line.

Tag play at second base — same low-angle, close-proximity framing, just a different base.

Category 5

Outfield Cam (Left / Right / Center Wall)

Position
Near outfield fence/wall, left/right/center.
Purpose
Fly ball calls, wall plays (robbed home runs), home run confirmation.
Tag when
Ball airborne over outfield, or outfielder playing near the wall.
Example

Outfielder tracking and catching a fly ball down the foul line, warning track visible.

Category 6

Dugout / Bullpen Cam

Position
Bench area or bullpen, fixed or handheld.
Purpose
Player/manager reactions, strategy discussion, celebrations.
Tag when
Subject is inside dugout/bullpen, not on the field of play.
Example

Players inside the dugout, not visible on the field of play.

Category 7

Crane / Aerial (Drone)

Position
Above the stadium.
Purpose
Opening shots, inning transitions, stadium/crowd atmosphere — and, increasingly, live-play overhead angles (e.g. a "sky cam" shot directly above home plate during an at-bat).
Tag when
Elevated overhead shot from directly above the field, whether used as a transition insert or as a live-action angle during play.
Example

True overhead drone shot directly above home plate — batter, catcher, and plate all visible from straight above.

Category 8

Scenic / Establishing

Position
Wide side-angle from the stands level, typically down the foul line, wide enough to include the skyline or stadium exterior beyond the outfield wall.
Purpose
Establishes ballpark atmosphere and scale — not focused on formation or a specific play. Play may be incidentally visible (e.g. a runner at first) but is not the point of the shot.
Tag when
Skyline, stadium roof structure, or exterior architecture takes up a significant portion of the frame; foul pole often visible.
Example

Wide shot down the first-base line with the Minneapolis skyline visible beyond the outfield wall.
